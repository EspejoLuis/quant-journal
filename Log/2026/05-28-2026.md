## 📚 Anki

- Some context. There are many ways to learn. Spaced repetition could be very helpful when it comes to dealing with complex topics. Also practice is needed of course, but before doing something, it's also good to know the theory behind it. A good tool that can make spaced repetition easier is Anki. This app takes care of the spaced repetition times for you..and this is cool. I have many Anki cards. As of today, I have created 2,695 cards on different topics for quantitative finance (coding, books, stochastic process, probability, monte carlo simulations etc.).
I grouped the cards by topic or by book. Every once in a while I reset all the cards so I can start from scratch for a specific topic. Now I'm focusing on reviewing [[Modelling, Pricing, and Hedging Counterparty Credit Exposure: A Technical Guide]] [Available in Amazon](https://amzn.eu/d/07E0fFVZ). Basically, when I read a quantitative book, I create anki cards for each chapter and review those while keep reading the book. In the past, I was following a rigid structure: I was moving to another chapter only after the previous chapter was completely read and the corresponding anki cards created. Nowadays, I'm more inclined to read also other chapters not in order, highlight them, move to another chapter, go back to the previous chapter, make anki cards for a chapter I left behind months ago and so on. I understood that learning is not linear, having this scattered structure for learning helps in understanding better the concepts in the long term. It gives the brain time to think about what has been learned, if it was clear or maybe why it was not clear, maybe there was a concept that was missing. It helps to identify what to do next.
