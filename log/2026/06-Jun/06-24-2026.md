## рЯФС Key Points

- **`{}` vs `()` for `std::vector` constructor:** `std::vector<double> v{n, val}` hits the `initializer_list<double>` constructor вАФ creates a 2-element vector. `std::vector<double> v(n, val)` calls the count+value constructor. Always use `()` for count+fill.
- **`const&` to a return value:** `const T& x = f()` extends the temporary's lifetime to `x`'s scope вАФ technically valid in C++, but fragile (breaks if `f` is changed to return a reference). Prefer `const T x = f()` and let NRVO avoid the copy.
- **Discounting in PDE is embedded вАФ no explicit $e^{-rT}$ needed:** the $-rV$ term in the BS PDE is the discounting. The $b_i = -2 \cdot \text{diffTerm} - r$ coefficient carries it continuously through every backward step. At $t=0$, `valuesCurrent` is already the present value.
- **CFL condition for explicit scheme:** $\Delta t \leq \frac{(\Delta S)^2}{\sigma^2 S_{\max}^2}$. The $C = 0.5$ from the heat equation stability criterion and the $\frac{1}{2}$ from the BS diffusion coefficient $\frac{\sigma^2 S^2}{2}$ cancel, giving the formula above. If violated, the solution blows up exponentially.
- **Neumann BC derivation:** as $S \to \infty$, $\Delta \to 1$ and $\Gamma \to 0$ вАФ the option value becomes linear in $S$. Setting the second derivative to zero at $S_{\max}$ reflects this: $\frac{\partial^2 V}{\partial S^2} = 0 \Rightarrow V[N] = 2V[N-1] - V[N-2]$. No knowledge of $K$ needed вАФ asymptotically exact for any product.
- **`std::swap` vs assignment for time-slice rolling:** `std::swap(v1, v2)` exchanges internal pointers in O(1); `v1 = v2` copies all elements in O(n). For a PDE with $N_S = 200$ nodes and $N_T = 1000$ time steps, swap saves 200,000 double copies per run.
