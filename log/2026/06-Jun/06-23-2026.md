## 🔑 Key Points

- **Forward/Backward Euler naming flips with direction of time:** when going backward in time (BS PDE, know $n+1$, want $n$): Forward Euler is implicit, Backward Euler is explicit. When going forward in time (know $n$, want $n+1$): Forward Euler is explicit, Backward Euler is implicit. The naming comes from where $L$ is evaluated relative to the index, not from explicit/implicit directly.
- **The unambiguous invariant:** $(I + \Delta t L)$ always multiplies the **known** vector; $(I - \Delta t L)$ always multiplies the **unknown** vector — regardless of time direction.
- **Four FD formulas for BS PDE (backward in time, know $n+1$, want $n$):**
  - Backward Euler (explicit): $(I + \Delta t L)\, V^{n+1} = V^n$
  - Forward Euler (implicit): $(I - \Delta t L)\, V^n = V^{n+1}$
  - CN: $(I - \frac{\Delta t}{2} L)\, V^n = (I + \frac{\Delta t}{2} L)\, V^{n+1}$
- **CFL stability condition for explicit scheme:** $\Delta t \leq \frac{(\Delta S)^2}{2}$. Halving $\Delta S$ requires $4\times$ more time steps — quadratic cost.
- **$S_{\max}$ from GBM 3-sigma rule:** $S_{\max} = S_0 \exp\!\left((r-q-\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2)T + 3\sigma\sqrt{T}\right)$. The $-\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2$ correction is the Itô correction from converting the log-process back to $S$.
- **L operator discretization coefficients (BS PDE, including dividends $q$):**
  - $a_i = \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 S_i^2\,\tfrac{1}{(\Delta S)^2} - (r-q)\,S_i\,\tfrac{1}{2\Delta S}$ (coefficient of $V_{i-1}$)
  - $b_i = -\sigma^2 S_i^2\,\tfrac{1}{(\Delta S)^2} - r$ (coefficient of $V_i$)
  - $c_i = \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2 S_i^2\,\tfrac{1}{(\Delta S)^2} + (r-q)\,S_i\,\tfrac{1}{2\Delta S}$ (coefficient of $V_{i+1}$)
